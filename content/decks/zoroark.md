### Inclusions

- Zekrom is too important to play just one. Darmanitan is great in enough matchups to warrant its inclusion. It also makes the deck fundamentally a lot stronger and threatening. The Pokemon lineup is seems pretty straightforward.
- Third Boss is included because I found myself always wanting to spam Boss. This deck is more aggressive before, so it won’t often be behind in prizes and able to use Counter Catcher.
- Black Belt’s Training is nice utility and helps reach key numbers. With lots of draw power and Pokegear, it’s not too hard to find. It has a lot of synergy with this deck.
- Three N’s PP Up is needed to maintain constant aggression and chain attackers. Whether you’re using Darmanitan’s or Zekrom’s attacks, you’ll need to use multiple PP Up each game.
- Counter Catcher is completely broken when your opponent takes the lead. Although you’ll be ahead a lot of the time, this card singlehandedly spikes the power ceiling of the deck and allows you to do disgusting things. It also makes making comebacks trivial.
- Pokegear 3.0 has been very handy. It helps consistency for Lillie, power for Boss, and late-game utility and synergy to easily reach for Iono or Black Belt. This deck relies on early Lillie as well as general Boss spam, and the card works well with Zoroark’s engine.
- The Pokemon search split is very awkward. All the search cards are good for general consistency. PokePad helps get Darmanitan online, Nest Ball makes finding Pecharunt realistic, and Ultra Ball is for safety. Ultra Ball’s downside is extremely significant in this deck, so that’s why there’s only one.
- TM: Evolution has been extremely helpful as there is a decent chance of finding it by Turn 1 or Turn 2. Even if you don’t, as long as you find Zoroark, it’s fine. It makes Secret Box and Lillie way better in the early-game. I think it’s better than Cyrano.
- Binding Mochi with Zekrom’s attack is broken. I tried playing only one and had trouble finding it consistently. It’s a little too important.
- Air Ballon is excellent utility as this deck constantly needs to pivot around due to the restrictions on the attacks and N’s PP Up. Also works well with Pecharunt and Secret Box. I would consider a second N’s Castle over the Balloon.
- N’s Castle is a great mobility card but has restrictions as a Stadium and can be bumped by the opponent, so it’s not super reliable. Typically best when you want to bump a troublesome Stadium or reuse a Mochi Zoroark.
- Team Rocket’s Watchtower singlehandedly carries the DawnZard matchup and also helps in some random difficult matchups.
- Artazon is for utility, consistency, and helping get a fast Darumaka, which can be annoying sometimes.
- Reversal Energy is honestly not that good but it is strong against Gholdengo. With seeing so many cards and potentially making big combos, having the option in general is not bad. This card is probably cuttable though.
- I am considering adding a second Pecharunt ex because it is just that good. You also need it to not be prized most of the time.

### Exclusions

- Toedscruel is a thing of the past. The Darmanitan build is now about equally as good against Gholdengo thanks to Zekrom’s support, and much better against everything else.
- Tatsugiri theoretically helps consistency by playing more games. However I did not get value from it once in lots of testing because it is very hard for everything to line up for it. It also occupies a precious board spot.
- Reshiram is not needed. Even if they swing into Zoroark, dealing massive damage with Zekrom is fine. We also have no board space to spare.
- Professor Turo’s Scenario seems like it would be good but I was basically never using it. This deck is a lot more fast and aggressive now and you don’t need Turo anymore.
- Similar reasoning applies to Pal Pad. Pokegear and a third Boss are much better suited for the deck in its current iteration, and they make the deck run a lot smoother and more consistent.
- Defiance Band seems pretty good but it is very situational. I tried one Mochi and one Band and I think two Mochi is better, but it is still close.

## Gameplay

- We need to be very careful with our board and resources! With no Turo or Pal Pad, there are no bailout options. This is completely fine, just need to be very judicious and careful with what Pokemon we’re putting down and not discarding Supporters besides Lillie.
- Choose to go second blind. Starting Zekrom when going second is usually fine but it is situation/matchup dependent. Starting Zorua when going second and attaching to it is fine if the matchup is one that probably won’t KO it on Turn 2, such as Dragapult.
- When going first, starting with Zorua and attaching to it is generally good, except against Gholdengo or any deck that can consistently KO it on turn 1.
- Trading away the extra Zekrom can punish you! There is a lot of pressure on our two Night Stretcher as is, so if you’re going to need more Zekrom attacks, try to keep it around. As the game goes on, it’s easier to trade away resources as you can map out exactly what you’ll need to close it out.
- Be aggressive. Win the prize trade. If you’re behind by two prizes, utilize Iono, CC, and Darmanitan to swing it back.
- Attaching Energy to Pecharunt in the late-game is something you should keep in mind. It comes up more than I expected.
- Pecharunt is a really big liability but you pretty much need it in play all the time anyway, so just try to win the prize race.
- Against decks with Item-lock or Eri, consider playing Pokegear (and Items in general) preemptively. Otherwise, hold it.
- Air Balloon is usually best on Zoroark or Fez due to synergy with Pecharunt.
- Try to avoid Munkidori damage setups from opponents. This can be done by using your own Munkidori for healing or by not leaving damage on the opponent’s board. Without Turo we have to be a little more careful about opposing Munkidori.
- Zorua’s Scratch with Binding Mochi can KO various Pokemon on Turn 1, including Munkidori!

## Matchups

### Gholdengo - Even

- Getting the lead in the prize exchange is basically instant win. There are so many ways to get two prizes in this matchup. If they aren’t pressuring you, you don’t necessarily need Zoroark right away, but they usually are.
- Make sure you always have a bench spot for Zekrom! It is usually more important than second Darm.
- The second Darm generally isn’t needed and you shouldn’t commit the board space to it. However the key exception is in the early-game if they are threatening a single-prize board. If they’re able to KO your only Darumaka with Solrock and have no two-prizers on the board, it’s a disaster. If that is possible, having two Darumaka is fine.
- Ideal board is double Zoroark, Zekrom, Darmanitan, Pecharunt, Fez (or Zoroark).
- Darmanitan is mostly used for Back Draft to one-shot a Gholdengo. Black Belt is important because they could KO Pecharunt with Gholdengo and then you need Black Belt to get the KO. The Reversal plus Black Belt three prize turn rarely lines up. It should be considered a nice treat, and is NOT part of the main strategy. It can also be a way to make a comeback if the opponent leaves themselves open to it.
- You will be taking a LOT of prize cards with Zekrom’s attack. Even more than Darmanitan, probably.
- Mochi Zoroark Flamebody Cannon is a great way to get those first two prize cards vs an active Solrock and benched Gimmighoul.
- Random extra 90 damage on Gholdengo can be utilized in many different ways. If you’re using Flamebody Cannon and don’t have a good spot to put that extra 90 damage, putting it on Gholdengo is usually best.
- Gusts are premium! Do not discard them!
- In the mid- and late-game, make sure you have a board that cannot be KO’d by Solrock! No more Zorua (or Darumaka if they have damage mods left)! Fezandipiti usually occupies the board instead of an extra Zoroark for this reason. Of course, make sure you also don’t run out of attackers. Remember that Darmanitan can always take the KO if they do go after Zoroark, making the two a multi-threat.

### Dragapult - Slightly Favorable

- Bench space is very weird and awkward. We typically want Munkidori, Zekrom, Darmanitan, double Zoroark, and Pecharunt. However, sometimes concessions need to be made. Going for triple Zorua on T1/T2 is still fine. Sometimes we don’t need Pecharunt, and it can be a liability. Sometimes Munkidori is hard to find. Juggling the board space is tricky and requires a delicate touch. Try to quantify the situation and recognize what’s needed and what isn’t.
- Darmanitan is very strong, especially in the early-game. It applies pressure and ensures that they cannot do whatever they want. It can deny Dusclops or take out two Drakloak at once, depending on the situation which is better. Since you cannot chain Darmanitan under Item-lock, it’s sometimes better to wait for a Boss, but it just depends.
- In general, being aggressive is good!
- You might be expecting to get the big combo to one-shot a Dragapult, but that doesn’t often happen. That is totally fine! Smacking the Dragapult for a ton of damage is still good! Hitting it for 290 sets it up for Adrenabrain. Hitting for any big amount sets up for a 3-prize Flamebody Cannon play. Just don’t forget that you cannot snipe Dragapult on the bench due to being Tera.

### Gardevoir - Favorable

- Even if you can’t KO the Diancie, smacking it for a bunch of damage is generally good for multiple reasons. If you deal enough to it, you can even finish it off with a Darmanitan snipe play.
- Go fast and apply as much pressure as possible.
- Watch out for Adrenabrain damage setups. Your own Adrenabrain plus the potential to remove their Munkidori from play can help with this. KO’ing two Munkidori at once isn’t hard if you have Adrenabrain available or if they leave damage on a Munki.
- Darmanitan’s attack can do nasty things, especially considering damage modifiers and their weakness to Dark. It’s not even out of the question to one-shot a Gardevoir and snipe something else with one attack!

### Absol - Favorable

- Be aggressive and bash their face in with Zekrom. Be aware that they may try to run you out of Zekrom so be sure to keep the second one around.
- Don’t swing into their Mega for no reason as they can punish you with Adrenabrain. If they aren’t fully set up or are boardlocked out of multiple Munki, it can be fine. It just depends on the situation.
- If they are playing Toedscruel, you may want to keep Darmanitan around. With Reversal Energy or Munkidori, it’s possible to KO Toedscruel and Toedscool in one attack.
- Even if they get Toedscruel, still keep Night Stretcher around as you may end up KO’ing their Toedscruel.
- Powering up Pecharunt is another way to possibly close out the game without access to Zekrom.
- If they are playing Cornerstone, use Boss to get around it. Each Boss should either take 2-3 prizes OR KO a Munki with a Dark. With Adrenabrain, you can even KO two Munki at once thanks to Darmanitan’s attack.
- If they have Cornerstone with no liabilities on the bench, it’s still no problem. Use Iono when they have a big hand, and use Zekrom’s Shred with various damage modifiers to take out the Cornerstone.

### Charizard / Noctowl - Favorable

- Watchtower is the key to this matchup. Slamming it early can be fine if they don’t have easy access to a punish, or you can wait until they play an Area Zero Underdepths. If I’m going first and they start with the Blazing Destruction Charmander, I’ll probably hold off on slamming Watchtower. If they start with Fan Rotom, it can KO Zorua on Turn 1. These factors may have me hesitant to play Watchtower Turn 1, but if there’s no clear reason not to, it’s fine to play. It’s also reliable to play to counter an Area Zero later, especially combined with Iono.
- Charizard can be extremely dangerous if they are allowed to play the game uninhibited. The best strategy is to try and scam them out of the game. Darmanitan, Watchtower, and Iono are all great for this.
- If they have Duskull, it’s only a real threat if they can combine it with an attacker. If you can clear or disrupt them to the point where they possibly can’t attack, then the Duskull isn’t an issue. If they are able to attack with Charizard, clearing their Duskull is generally good because it hard caps Charizard’s damage. They have a hard time actually KO’ing Zoroark in the early- and mid-game, so use that to your advantage.
- You don’t necessarily have to deal with their attackers either. Try to take prize cards wherever you can. Sometimes you can race them while ignoring Charizard entirely, as it is somewhat hard to KO. In other words, stop them from attacking if you can. If not, cripple their support.

### Charizard / Secret Box - Unfavorable

- They have more outs to Watchtower, so yolo slamming it isn’t as good (but it depends on the situation). Try to combo it with Iono or use it to bump one of their Stadiums.
- Similar to Dawn Zard, the Duskull is only a threat along with an attacker. Try to deny them attackers by wiping Charmanders with Darmanitan. If you can’t stop Charizard from coming into play, try to remove Duskull to stop them from one-shotting Zoroark with Charizard. 
- Keep Pecharunt off the board in the early-game to make it hard for them to take prizes.
- Make sure that you don’t have anything on the board that Pidgeot can KO in the late-game. This only matters if you’re KO’ing their Charizard, but I found it to be relevant.
- Sometimes you can one-shot Charizard or Pidgeot. Whether you can and what’s correct just depends on the situation.

### Grimmsnarl - Unfavorable

- This matchup is all about speed and pressure. Use Darmanitan to clear off Froslass and/or Munkidori. Froslass is usually the default target because they’re harder to set up. However, KO’ing double Munki can stop them from doing certain plays. For example, if you take out both Munki, they cannot snipe KO a Zorua, which is relevant.
- Staggering Zoroark/Zorua can play around Froslass. However, if they are threatening double Munki plus Grimmsnarl, they’ll just snipe KO the Zorua, which is even worse.
- Watch out for Devolution board wipe. The main way to counter this is to attach to Pecharunt so that you have something to close out the game with, but of course that only matters if you are low enough on prize cards. Pecharunt is also quite the liability. Constantly benching fresh Zorua can also play around Devo, but feeding them to the slaughter. All of these things are very situational but honestly it’s very difficult to navigate and win.
- Iono can cripple them in the late-game and potentially scam them to miss the Devo. Watchtower to shut off Ursaluna can also be relevant.
- Munkidori is obviously very good to have, but it can be hard to keep up with Energy attachments while using Darmanitan. As a result, N’s PP Up are premium resources.
- Zekrom to KO Grimmsnarl is sometimes the best available play, though I’d rather use Darmanitan to KO two Pokemon if that’s possible.
- If they play Budew, try to play around it by having backup Zoroark with an Energy when using Darmanitan, or not using Darmanitan if you won’t be able to get a follow-up attack.

### Zoroark Mirror - Even

I assume that the matchup is favored against the Toedscruel version because they cannot punish a single-prize board and have to put the first two-prize Pokemon in play due to the Darmanitan threat.

- Make sure that your board is never vulnerable to Darmanitan! Conversely, punish them with Darmanitan if you can.
- If you’re on odd prizes and only behind one prize, Darmanitan can be used as a single-prize attacker to fix the prize trade, even if it’s only getting one prize. Zekrom itself can even be used as a single-prize attacker to KO Zorua, but that’s only if you’re truly desperate.
- Just take two prizes whenever you can and try to get ahead on the prize race. If they have no Darumaka in play, your single-prize board is safe and you can wait for them to get the first two-prize Pokemon in play.
- If they are threatening Toedscruel to lock out Zekrom, you can use Pecharunt to close out the game against their Pecharunt/Fez. If Toedscruel is threatening to shut you out of the game, you’ll have to KO Toedscruel. Reversal Energy can potentially be good here, and so is Adrenabrain if you somehow have damage on board.

### Kangaskhan / Bouffalant - Slightly Unfavorable

- Be careful about when you smack into their Kang. If their Kang won’t have enough Energy to use Jumbo Ice Cream, or if you’re slamming them with Iono Watchtower, those are good times to hit into it. If their board is well-established, they have Munkis with Darks, and/or a large hand, those are times to NOT hit into their Kang, as you’ll get punished.
- If you’re in a situation where hitting their Kang is bad, just Boss KO something and wait for Iono Watchtower.
- Giving them Adrenabrain damage can be risky as they can pivot into rushing prize cards (especially if they play Iron Leaves).
- Early-game, swinging into whatever their active is is generally fine. Don’t burn through too many Bosses, but strategically Bossing around a threatening Kang can be good. Boss is a premium resource!
- Slamming Watchtower on Turn 1 is generally fine as it can sometimes win the game on the spot. Otherwise, save it for the Iono combo and use that to win.
