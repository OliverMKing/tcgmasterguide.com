# Before the Tournament

Proper preparation is essential for tournament success. Practice your deck extensively and understand all possible matchups.

## What to Bring

Tournament essentials:

- **Deck** registered and sleeved with opaque sleeves
- **Pen and paper** for tracking life and damage
- **Dice or damage counters** for tracking effects
- **Water and snacks** for long tournament days
- **Playmat** (recommended for card protection)
- **Extra sleeves** in case of damage

## Deck Preparation

### Registration Requirements

- Exactly 60 cards in your main deck
- All cards must be legal for the format
- Sleeves must be opaque and uniform
- Have a decklist ready to submit

### Practice Routine

1. **Know your deck inside out** - Memorize key combos
2. **Test common matchups** - Understand win conditions
3. **Practice mulligans** - Know when to take them
4. **Time management** - Play within time limits

## Mental Preparation

Stay focused and calm during matches:

- Take time between rounds to rest
- Review your plays and learn from losses
- Stay positive and friendly
- Don't tilt from bad luck

## During the Tournament

### Game Management

- Shuffle thoroughly and offer cuts
- Track game state carefully
- Call judges when needed
- Maintain good sportsmanship

### Time Management

- Play at a reasonable pace
- Don't rush important decisions
- Be aware of round time limits
- Practice good clock management

## After Each Round

1. **Record your results** - Keep track of opponents and outcomes
2. **Take notes** - What worked, what didn't
3. **Hydrate and rest** - Stay fresh for later rounds
4. **Stay positive** - One loss doesn't end your tournament

## Tournament Etiquette

- Arrive early for registration
- Be respectful to all players
- Follow judge rulings
- Report match results promptly
- Have fun and make friends!
